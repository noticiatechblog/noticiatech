*Over the past three years, the artificial intelligence race has been dominated by increasingly powerful models and billions of dollars in infrastructure investments. Now, a new competition is beginning to take shape: control of the interface that connects people and businesses to AI systems. Recent moves by OpenAI suggest that the next battle will not be fought solely over models, but over the devices that serve as the gateway to this technology.*

## OpenAI Wants to Control the Entire Artificial Intelligence Experience

**OpenAI** is moving beyond model development and seeking to participate directly in the interaction layer between users and artificial intelligence.

![OpenAI and AI hardware](imagem1.webp)

*The market is beginning to shift from competition over models to competition over interfaces and devices.*

Historically, software companies have relied on third-party platforms to distribute their products. In the AI era, this means depending on smartphones, browsers, and operating systems controlled by companies such as **Apple**, **Google**, and **Microsoft**.

### Why Does This Matter?

Whoever controls the interface controls distribution.

This is the same logic that transformed operating systems into massive business platforms during the eras of personal computers and smartphones.

### The Signal OpenAI Is Sending

Recent developments indicate that the organization led by **Sam Altman** does not intend to remain merely a provider of AI models.

Instead, the company appears determined to build its own experience layer, reducing external dependencies and creating a direct connection between users and AI agents.

## The AI Race Is Shifting from Models to Interfaces

The first phase of generative AI was defined by competition between models.

![Evolution of the AI race](imagem2.webp)

*The industry's focus is moving from AI models to how users access artificial intelligence.*

Companies competed on performance, reasoning capabilities, and response quality.

### The Second Phase Was Infrastructure

The explosion in AI demand triggered unprecedented investments in data centers, chips, and computing capacity.

Companies such as **Nvidia**, **Microsoft**, **Amazon**, and **Google** began competing to build the infrastructure required to support increasingly advanced models.

### The Third Phase Will Be the Interface

A new strategic question is now emerging:

How will people interact with AI every day?

The answer could redefine the technology industry over the next decade.

Just as smartphones replaced multiple previous technologies, devices designed specifically for artificial intelligence could create an entirely new category of digital products.

## What Apple, Google, Meta, and OpenAI Are Fighting For

Today's competition goes far beyond hardware.

It involves attention, data, user relationships, and control over the digital experience.

![Competition among AI giants](imagem3.webp)

*Major technology companies are competing to control the primary access point to artificial intelligence.*

### Apple's Role

**Apple** possesses one of the strongest competitive advantages in technology: billions of active devices distributed worldwide.

Its strategy focuses on integrating AI directly into its ecosystem, using the iPhone, iPad, and Mac as central access points.

### Google's Role

**Google** is attempting to transform traditional search into a conversational AI experience.

This transition is already visible through the expansion of AI Overviews and AI-assisted search experiences.

The trend connects directly to the movement explored in [The Interface War Has Begun: Why Apple, OpenAI, Google, and Microsoft Want to Replace Apps with AI Agents](https://noticiatech.com.br/ia/a-guerra-das-interfaces-comecou-por-que-apple-openai-google-e-microsoft-querem-substituir-os-aplicativos-por-agentes-de-ia/).

### OpenAI's Role

**OpenAI** is pursuing a different path.

Rather than relying exclusively on existing platforms, the company appears interested in helping build the next generation of digital interfaces.

This strategy expands its ability to capture value and strengthen its competitive position.

## What This Means for Businesses and the Future of Computing

The transformation underway affects more than consumers.

Businesses may also be significantly impacted by the evolution of AI-native devices.

### AI Agents Will Become More Present

The trend suggests that intelligent agents will move beyond functioning as standalone software tools.

Instead, they may become permanent layers of interaction, assisting professionals with operational tasks, strategic decisions, and business workflows.

This development aligns with trends discussed in [AI First: Why This Strategy Is Redefining Business Competitiveness](https://noticiatech.com.br/ia/o-que-e-ai-first-e-por-que-essa-estrategia-esta-redefinindo-a-competitividade-das-empresas/).

### The Device May No Longer Be the Center

For decades, computers and smartphones have served as the primary means of digital interaction.

Artificial intelligence introduces the possibility of a different model.

Instead of opening applications to perform specific tasks, users may simply interact with agents capable of executing multiple actions autonomously.

This shift represents far more than a technological upgrade.

It points toward a potential redefinition of computing itself.

OpenAI's recent moves are significant because they suggest that the world's largest technology companies are already positioning themselves for this next phase. Competition for the best AI models remains important, but the next major winner may be the company that controls the interface connecting billions of people to artificial intelligence every day.
